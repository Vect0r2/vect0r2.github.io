Guide to the Supermatter

This guide serves as general instruction on the basics of running a supermatter engine, hereby referred to as the SM. Basic atmospherics knowledge is recommended.

The Basics

Cooling

The most important aspect of any SM setup is the cooling loop, as the SM constantly makes heat and waste products. Most cooling loops manage both issues by injecting a cold working gas, typically N2, while siphoning all gas created in the chamber. [picture]

This is a typical SM cooling setup. The bad air from the SM chamber is siphoned using the scrubbers on the right side into the orange pipes, cooled using the 4 freezers on the right, and connected to 2 filters on the left. The first filter is set for N2, looping the cooled nitrogen back to the cyan line into SM chamber. The rest is sent to the second filter, which has no filter set, which is sent to the red scrubber network pipe to be disposed of.

Radiation Collection

One of the main reasons to run an SM is to generate radiation for power production, despite the health drawbacks to yourself, your crewmates, and your captain. Radiation collector arrays use plasma tanks to generate power from the SM's radiation, and distribute that power to a connected power node.

[picture]

This is a typical SM radiation collection setup. The radiation from the SM is collected and converted into power by these collectors, and sent to the yellow power network. This power is then buffered in the connected SMES, and sent down the red wire to the rest of the ship.

Radiation Generation

An important step to power generation, the SM does not make much radiation with just a cooling loop. There are several methods to increase the radiation output, mainly using emitters to shoot lasers at the SM. Emitters fire a hitscan laser, which increases the power level of the SM, thereby increasing the SM's radiation output and thus power production. Reflectors can be used to redirect several emitter beams into the SM. Other ways to increase SM power level are introducing certain gases, such as oxygen and plasma, and throwing items or people into the SM [ADMINS KNOW WHEN YOU DO THIS. DO NOT MAKE PILL FACTORY SUPERMATTERS.].

[picture]

This is a typical SM emitter array, utilizing several single reflectors and a reflector box to direct emitter beams into the SM. This emitter array is powered on the cyan wire, using a buffered SMES from the main line. This provides backup power in the case that the SM is shutdown for a long duration.

Atmospherics Tips

  • Ctrl clicking atmospherics machines (pumps, freezers) will often toggle them on or off.
  • Alt clicking atmospherics machines (pumps, freezers) will often maximize their settings.
  • When using directional (thin) windows, do not place the window on the same tile as an expected high pressure area.
  • SMs decay very slowly when in a vacuum, which may buy you more time than desperately dumping a hot working gas into the chamber.
  • Atmospherics stops moving when a ship moves, meaning the SM will build up heat and waste gas on it's tile. It may be of value to disable emitters during this process.
