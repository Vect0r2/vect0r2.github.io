Lugol-class GEC Engineering Project
Basic Information
Short Name: Lugol-class
Manufacturer: Unknown
Faction: Unspecified (Prefix: XSV)
Crew Size: 11
Size: Large
Tags: Engineering, Construction
Buyable: No
Description

The Lugol is effectively an enormous Galactic Engineers Concordat research barge, used as a test bed for refinements to power systems, new technologies, and so on. As it offers freedom from the usual constraints of working aboard vessels belonging to other Syndicate factions, Lugols are especially popular among the GEC’s more radical members. Accordingly, they have a reputation for either accomplishing the impossible or generating the equivalent of a new star when they inevitably melt down. Lugols are generally only found on the Frontier, where the collateral damage from potential accidents can be kept to a minimum and secrecy, when needed, can be better maintained.

The Lugol is what you get when you give a GEC eccentric a vast budget, and a vaster imagination. Well-equipped to house all kinds of engineering projects, on varying degrees of insanity.

Gameplay

The Lugol-class GEC Engineering Project, as its name implies, is designed for engineering projects - its most notable feature being a large empty space in its fore. This space is designed for all kinds of engineering projects, from singularities to cement-fueled supermatters. Within the construction area, is a tight storage space containing all you need for your engineering endeavors. This includes:

  • 100 metal, 150 glass, 50 metal rods, 20 reinforced plasma glass, 50 wooden planks, 20 plastitanium, 20 plasteel and 50 plastic
  • 4 emitter boards, 4 tesla coil boards, 4 radiation collector boards, 4 grounding rod boards, 4 thermomachine boards, 2 SMES boards
  • An RPED
  • 2 boxes of stock parts, 5 high capacity power cells
  • 3 industrial RCDs, 3 large matter cartridges
  • 4 field generators, a singularity generator, a tesla generator, and an unassembled particle accelerator
  • 2 nitrogen canisters, 1 plasma canister

The Lugol also features full engineering R&D, and a large atmos area. The atmos room contains all base gases, a mixing chamber, and an atmos harvester - linked together with a complex series of pipes. The atmos department is also geared to deal with dangerous engineering catastrophes, with firesuits, holofans, large scrubbers, advanced extinguishers, and no sense of self-preservation.

What Roles?

The roles that can be selected aboard the Lugol-class are as follows:

  • 1 Project Leader
  • 3 GEC Engineers
  • 3 Atmospheric Technicians
  • 1 Beverage Specialist
  • 1 Shaft Miner
  • 2 GEC Apprentices

How Should I Pilot This?

The Lugol is best suited for low-temperature planets with a wide variety of gases, to make the most use out of its atmos harvester, and any heat exchanging contraptions your engineers might construct. Safety-minded Project Leaders may seek to trade with medical vessels for more sophisticated radiation medicine. The Lugol is not equipped for fighting, and its abundant resources and limited mobility make it a lucrative pick for pirates. Should the threat of pirates arise, consider hiring Inteq mercenaries to defend your ship - or booby trap it with disposal traps and burning plasma.
